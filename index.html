Bears

A bear is a type of mammal that belongs to the family Ursidae. There are eight species of bears found in the Northern Hemisphere, including the polar bear, giant panda, and six other species. Bears are characterized by their distinctive physical features, such as their shaggy fur, powerful build, and distinctive snout.

Giraffes

A giraffe is a large, even-toed ungulate mammal that belongs to the family Giraffidae. It is the tallest mammal on Earth, with some males reaching heights of over 18 feet (5.5 meters) tall! Giraffes are native to Africa, specifically in savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands.

Lions

A lion is a large, powerfully built cat that is second in size only to the tiger. It is a famous apex predator, also known as the “king of the beasts.” Lions are known for their majestic appearance, distinctive mane, and loud roar, which can be heard from several miles away.

Monkeys

A monkey is a type of primate mammal that belongs to the infraorder Simiiformes, also known as simians. Monkeys are characterized by their agility, intelligence, and social behavior. They are found in various parts of the world, including Africa, Asia, and the Americas

Alligators

An alligator is a large reptile that belongs to the genus Alligator and the family Alligatoridae. There are two extant species of alligators: the American alligator (Alligator mississippiensis) and the Chinese alligator (Alligator sinensis). Alligators are semi-aquatic carnivorous reptiles with four small legs and a very large, long tail. The tail is half the animal’s total length and helps propel them rapidly through the water and create pools of water during dry seasons called “gator holes”.
